ABOUT OUR FOUNDER

Dr. Bharat Thakur is the founder of Artistic Yoga a dynamic form of yoga that combines modern principles of fitness with ancient yogic techniques, with the goal of transforming a person at all levels, physical as well as mental and emotional. He is largely responsible for popularising this energetic and vigorous form of yoga from year 2000 onwards in the major metropolitan cities of India as well as in Dubai, while Power Yoga and Ashtanga Yoga were gaining popularity in the west. His taught several well-known celebrities including Kareena Kapoor, Salman Khan, industrialists Sunil Mittal and Ratan Tata, as well as international sportspersons like Boris Becker and Shane Warne.

He married the famous Bollywood actress Bhumika Chawla in 2008 and briefly dabbled in films, and subsequently farming, which he continues to develop as a passion. He is also a prolific painter, with several solo and group exhibitions to his credit. He is the author of several book on yoga.

He was featured on the cover of India Today, listed among the 'Top 50 Young Guns - Leaders of This Generation,' featured again in Time Magazine where he was invited to write a guest feature. He has conducted yoga, spirituality and meditation workshops in the US, UK, Italy, Germany, Thailand, Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia, Azerbaijan, Bali and Australia. He continues to conduct retreats where he takes a small group of students through an intense meditation program that transforms a person in a way that is life-changing in every way.

An unconventional personality, Bharat Thakur has a small but fiercely loyal group of students who embrace his idiosyncratic and paradoxical approach to spirituality. He refuses to be pigeonholed, rejecting ashrams and followers of all kinds. He believes that the student-teacher relationship is personal and unique and cannot be packaged or commoditised. Despite his close circuit of friends and well-known personalities in all walks of life, he claims to be a catalyst; completely uninvolved in his students processes but always available to them when required. He prefers to lead a normal life, pursuing his many passions with an obsessive energy that permeates all aspects of his life.

In Conversation With Mr. Bharat Thakur

“ I don't believe in arguments. They are a waste of time. I prefer method. Method is something you cannot think about or discuss. It will prove itself in a certain span of time. The right method makes things happen. “

For a spiritual master to develop a form of Yoga that believes as much in weight loss as it does in meditation is confusing. Does one have to have a six-pack in order to reach enlightenment?

"I don't see the problem. Can a man with a headache or backache sit for meditation? Impossible. He will only think about the part of his body aching. The art of Yoga is to make the whole body invisible. By invisible I mean that you should not be able to feel it. If you can feel a joint or an organ or your head it means you have a problem. If you can't feel your knee or neck it means it is perfect. To enter into silence, the body should be free of disease. And that includes obesity."
